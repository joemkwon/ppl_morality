# Human data — not included in the public release

The behavioural human-subjects data (the pilot moral-judgment ratings and the
raw Prolific survey) is **deliberately not part of this public repository**. It
is held privately by the authors and is available on reasonable request for
research purposes, subject to the original consent terms.

This is a privacy decision: the raw jsPsych exports contained participant
identifiers (Prolific IDs, IP addresses, user agents), and to guarantee that
**no participant data of any kind is public**, no human-data file is shipped
here — not even a de-identified one.

## What this means for reproduction

- The model is **fully reproducible without the human data**. Every prior the
  model needs (per-goal utility/urgency means and SEs, community-frequency
  estimates) is reproduced verbatim — exactly as reported in the paper — as
  documented constants in
  [`src/moral_rules/priors.py`](../../src/moral_rules/priors.py).
- `experiments/compare_model_human.py` will detect the absent data file and
  skip gracefully. To run the model↔human overlay yourself, place your private
  `Experiment2_Pilot.csv` (columns: `Goal`, `Steps on Grass`, `Moral Judgment`,
  `Sign`) in this directory.
- The paper's Figure 3 (community goal distributions) and Figure 5 (priors by
  sign condition) are plots of that raw survey and are therefore documented
  here but not regenerated by this release.
